Purpose

To build and own the operating architecture that lets PowerVox run predictably without living in Rachel's head — and to turn that same capability into one of the most valuable things PowerVox does for clients. Scott is the architect: the person who makes structure visible, makes it work, and surfaces the questions the senior team needs to answer. Scott is the backbone of PowerVox.

Why this shape

A large public-sector engagement — an interactive target operating model with decision tools, playbooks, personas, pathways and swim lanes — is a far truer description of what Scott excels at than "operations" ever was. The same five-question method (why at the centre, then what, when, how, who) was being drawn by hand on flip charts years ago; the sophistication of the output changed, not the discipline. The role is explicitly back-room by design: presenting, facilitating, speaking and selling are not his remit and never silently become his. His founder-focus role runs in the peer's register — the co-founding partner who can say "that breaks the diary" as an equal. Trust built through many years of work together, since the Serenwood days. On a path toward equity, with independent advice required on anything affecting his own stake — a designed feature, not an accident.
