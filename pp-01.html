Position Paper PP-01 · v0.1 · May 2026

Voice Intelligence and Voice Quotient™: faculty and measure

Adopted (internal) · claim held at hypothesis until the beta validates

"Voice Intelligence is the human leadership capability. Voice Quotient™ is how we measure it. The same relationship as intelligence and IQ — the faculty and its quotient."

The position, stated plainly

PowerVox adopts a two-layer naming convention. Voice Intelligence is the leadership capability: whether what a leader thinks and intends actually lands in others, and whether they can receive what reaches them — the human-facing category name, the thing that in time sits alongside IQ and EQ. Voice Quotient™ (VQ) is the measure: the proprietary six-dimension instrument, the scores it produces, and any index derived from it — the sacred, trademarked, instrument-anchored noun.

The one-line test that prevents drift: if you can put a number on it, it is Voice Quotient; if you are naming the human capability itself, it is Voice Intelligence.

Why this position, and not the alternatives

Rejected — "Voice Quotient everywhere; retire Voice Intelligence." Clean, but it discards the IQ/EQ analogy that does real positioning work, and it contradicts the research programme, which is founded on the claim that Voice Intelligence sits alongside IQ and EQ. A category cannot be founded on a noun it has just retired.

Rejected — "Leave it ambiguous; use either." The status quo until now, and precisely the vocabulary drift the Construct exists to prevent. Ambiguity in a category-founding vocabulary is not neutral — it is corrosive, because founding documents are cited for their definitions long after their findings are superseded.

Adopted — "Faculty and measure." The only option under which both governing documents are simultaneously correct: the canonical language keeps Voice Quotient™ as its sacred noun; the research programme keeps Voice Intelligence as the category it founds. The contradiction dissolves because the two were never competing — and no one finds "intelligence versus IQ" confusing. It also settles the future enterprise instrument: it is a Voice Quotient index — a measure — never a "Voice Intelligence Index". An index is a number; numbers are Voice Quotient.

The cost, named honestly

This adds a second protected term to a canon whose whole discipline is restraint. Accepted on one condition: Voice Intelligence is strictly the faculty-level name for the capability Voice Quotient™ measures — never a free-floating second sacred noun to be decorated with sub-terms. The one-line test is the fence. If the team finds itself coining "Voice Intelligence" compounds that are not simply the human-readable form of a Voice Quotient measure, the fence has been breached and this paper should be reopened.

The load-bearing distinction: the naming is adopted now; the claim it implies is not. "Voice Intelligence is a distinct, measurable leadership capability that sits alongside IQ and EQ" is a category claim of the highest order, and PowerVox has not yet earned the right to assert it publicly. We may use the names now. We may not yet make, in public, the category claim the names imply. The beta is what converts the second into a right we have earned.

What this paper governs

Anyone writing in the PowerVox voice — methodology, marketing, research, Enfys — uses this paper as the single reference for which noun to use when. It sits above the canonical language (which holds the sacred noun) and the research programme (which holds the claim), and states how they relate. Owner: Hannah (methodology and research integrity), with Rachel (category). A standing guardrail follows: Signal is depicted as central, never named as a peer intelligence.
