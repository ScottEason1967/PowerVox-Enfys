The Burrows

Every village runs on workings you cannot see from the green. These are the chassis's subsystems — the things the Cartographer has to actually build. One canonical entry each; the Gather's cells point here, and nowhere else.

How to read this. Each system carries its owner, the lifecycle stages it serves, what exists today, and what has to be built. Statuses are honest: most of these are still to design — that is the point of the register. When a system gains its own page or canonical document, the entry links to it and this page stops describing it.

The Gate

To build

Qualification criteria per product · intake route (form or referral script) · routing rules to diary or decline · a pipeline view the keepers can read at a glance.

⚑ Design v0.1 — Cartographer's proposal
The flow
  1. Enquiry arrives — Substack, LinkedIn, Black Egg result page, speaking room, or warm referral. Source captured at entry, always (it is the funnel's learning).
  2. Intake form: role and seniority, organisation, which ware they're drawn to, timing, budget signal, how they found us.
  3. Routing rules per product: Root to Grow (senior leader · can fund £1,495 · cohort timing fits) · TOM engagement (organisational sponsor · ~£30k budget · three-month window) · Greenhouse (alumni only until the Year 2–3 direct-entry trigger) · speaking (diary rules apply).
  4. Qualified → a capped weekly allocation of conversation slots on Rachel's diary. Unqualified → nurture (Substack) or the decline template, kindly.
  5. Pipeline board, five stages: New → Qualified → Conversation → Proposal → Won/Lost — readable by all keepers at a glance, reviewed in the weekly focus check.
Tooling ⚑

Kajabi form as the single intake (Kajabi is the canonical platform; Mehtab configures); pipeline board in a shared sheet or Notion; capped booking via a scheduling tool wired to the Village Clock's slot taxonomy.

Artefacts to make
  • Qualification criteria sheet, one per ware
  • Decline + nurture email templates
  • The pipeline board itself
Failure modes this design must beat
  • Unqualified calls reaching Rachel's diary (the system's whole point)
  • Source attribution lost at entry
  • Pipeline invisible until someone asks

The flow

04The Village ClockScheduling & cadence protection⚑ designed v0.1 · confirm + implement

The visible/cave rhythm encoded into how everything is scheduled — whole-day batches, the protected cave-month for beta analysis, over-commitment flagged early. Energy is the binding constraint; this system is its guardian.

Owner: Scott, in the peer's register
Exists today: the 2/4 model as agreement, not as system

All stages

To build

Cadence rules in the calendar itself · capacity dashboard across the four seats · the over-commitment flag and the weekly focus check, kept light.

⚑ Design v0.1 — Cartographer's proposal
The rules, encoded
  1. Weekly: two build days per leader protected (a health guardrail) — slot type, not aspiration.
  2. Monthly: week four is the cave week — no client sessions booked into it, by calendar rule.
  3. Annually: the beta analysis is one protected cave-month, never scattered hours.
  4. Delivery caps: single cohort track in H1, parallel two-track only once grooved; three TOM engagements in the year; speaking capped. The caps live in the calendar, not in memory.
The mechanism

A slot taxonomy on the shared calendar (Delivery · Build · Cave · Signal) plus a one-glance capacity view: founder-days committed against cap, per month, per keeper. Any month breaching its cap raises the over-commitment flag — which is not an alert, it is the peer conversation, held in Scott's register ("that breaks the diary").

Artefacts
  • The slot taxonomy
  • The monthly capacity view
  • The weekly focus check agenda — three questions, kept light
Failure modes
  • The cave eroded by "just one call"
  • Aggregate commitment invisible until someone is exhausted
  • The flag fired as a system notification instead of a human conversation

The Harvest

To build

Capture instrument & storage under the Deep Vault's rules · the n-gates as actual checkpoints · analysis workspace for the cave-month.

⚑ Design v0.1 — Cartographer's proposal · gated on the Deep Vault review
The pipeline
  1. Capture: assessment platform (⚑ unchosen — deliberately, so the Vault's reviewer sees the processor before lock-in) takes the 38-question instrument + companion questionnaire.
  2. Identifier split: pseudonymisation within 30 days of capture, per the Vault — analysis never needs names.
  3. The research store: pseudonymised dataset, access-controlled, n-counts automated so the gates announce themselves (n=20 hygiene · n=60 mid-beta · ~n=100 close).
  4. The cave-month workspace: analysis against the pre-set criteria; findings to the Charter; Hannah's thresholds untouchable on commercial grounds.
  5. Class B lane: at each TOM engagement close, the pattern abstraction runs (the pattern-library schema) — organisation-unidentifiable patterns only into the library.
Failure modes

  • Identifiers leaking into working files
  • Gate counts tracked by memory instead of by the system

The Ledger

To build

Invoicing schedule per product · payment terms & chase process · Greenhouse renewal flow · the revenue view against the ~£225k year-one path.

⚑ Design v0.1 — Cartographer's proposal · this system had nothing beneath it
The flow
  1. Invoice schedules per ware: RtG — on enrolment, before cohort start. Greenhouse — monthly subscription, collected automatically. TOM — three instalments (proposal: on signature / month two / on handover). Speaking — 50% on booking, 50% on delivery. ⚑ All proposals.
  2. Terms: 14 days; the chase ladder at 7, 14 and 21 days overdue — first chase automated and friendly, the third is a keeper's phone call, because many clients are also friends and the system must spare the friendship by being impersonal early.
  3. The revenue view, monthly: actual vs the ~£225k worked path · the PowerVox-product share % (the single guiding metric, visible every month, not annually) · the Greenhouse run-rate tracker against the ~£52.5k exit target.
  4. Renewals: Greenhouse retention watched monthly against the ~85% assumption; annual membership review cycle.
Tooling ⚑

Kajabi payments for subscriptions; a proper accounting package for invoicing and the chase ladder; the revenue view as a one-page monthly sheet until it earns a dashboard.

Failure modes
  • The guiding metric computed once a year instead of living monthly
  • Chasing friends ad hoc and awkwardly
  • Run-rate (the asset) confused with in-year revenue (the symptom)
