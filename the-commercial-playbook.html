About this playbook

What the commercial playbook governs, the metric it serves, and the ladder it sells.

The guiding metric

One number governs the year: the PowerVox-product share of revenue, moving from ~53% in Year 1 to ~75% in Year 2 to ~89% in Year 3. Everything here is in service of that arc — a deliberate substitution of founder-led bespoke for scalable product, computed monthly, not annually.

The value ladder

One clean path: the Black Egg paid diagnostic (~£49–£99, the front door's paid step) → Root to Grow (£1,495, six-week cohort) → the Greenhouse (~£125/month recurring) → the TOM engagement / the Loop (~£30k, bespoke, capped and falling) → speaking & coaching (premium, founder-led, capped). One front door, one paid step, one core, one recurring engine, one premium pair.

North stars · what the plays deliver toward
  • For the buyer — a clear first step that proves value before a big commitment.
  • For PowerVox — product share rising, founder energy protected, evidence and revenue on one path.
  • For the category — claims that stay behind the evidence, so the wares are honest.
Where the How lives

This playbook is the manual — the commercial decisions, run play by play. How the work flows day to day is the operating model's How page, the Gather; the commercial subsystems (the Gate, the Welcome, the Ledger) sit in the Burrows; the wares themselves are in the Wares.

The open commercial decisions

The Loop trade name · the TOM/Loop price (~£30k) and capacity cap (three vs six) · the SME value-ladder line in or out · the Loop artefact reframe · the single front door confirmation · the Greenhouse direct-entry trigger · the ~£50k enterprise price as a 2030 destination · the first associate facilitator · the per-ware invoice schedules. Each sits inside the play it belongs to.

Set the direction · Play 1

Choose the commercial recipe

Route an opportunity to its commercial path — the ware that names which downstream plays run, and in what order.

Run this play when
  • A new enquiry or opportunity needs a route to market.
  • A new ware is being considered.
  • An existing line is re-evaluated and the route may shift.
How to run it · the five routes
  1. The Black Egg — the paid entry diagnostic; the scalable inbound route.
  2. Root to Grow — the six-week cohort; the front of the funnel and the beta vehicle.
  3. The Greenhouse — recurring membership; the destination and the recurring engine.
  4. The TOM engagement (the Loop) — bespoke operating-model delivery; capped and falling.
  5. Speaking & coaching — the premium pair; founder-led, every room routes back to the diagnostic.
  6. An opportunity can mix routes, but name the primary — it sets the dominant downstream play set.
Recommendation

Route toward the funnel and the guiding metric — prefer the paths that grow product share. Take bespoke (TOM) only within the cap, and only where it feeds the second engine.

Open decisions inside this play
  • Which ware a given enquiry routes to is a judgement until the qualification criteria are set (Play 5).

Set the direction · Play 2

Run the substitution arc

Run the year so PowerVox-product share rises decisively, trading founder time out of bespoke and into the funnel.

Run this play when
  • Continuously; reviewed monthly and at annual planning.
How to run it
  1. Hold the guiding metric — product share % — visible monthly.
  2. Protect founder time for product build, not bespoke drift.
  3. Drop bespoke only as the funnel covers it (Play 19, cap-and-drop).
  4. Each year, move the mix deliberately toward products (~53% → 75% → 89%).
Recommendation

It is a substitution, not an addition. Every bespoke engagement kept past its time slows the arc — the discipline is to let go as the product covers it.

Win the work · Play 5

Qualify & route a lead

Only warm, qualified conversations reach Rachel's diary; everyone else is routed kindly. The operational half of the funnel.

Run this play when
  • Every enquiry — from Substack, LinkedIn, a Black Egg result, a speaking room, or a referral.
How to run it
  1. Capture the source at entry, always — it is the funnel's learning.
  2. Qualify on role, seniority, budget signal and timing against per-product criteria.
  3. Route to the right ware (Play 1's recipe).
  4. Qualified → a capped weekly conversation slot on Rachel's diary.
  5. Unqualified → Substack nurture, or the decline template, kindly.
  6. Track on a five-stage pipeline board: New → Qualified → Conversation → Proposal → Won/Lost.
Recommendation

The diary cap is the whole point — protect it. Source attribution at entry is non-negotiable; it is how the funnel learns what works.

How to run it
  1. Invoice schedules per ware: RtG on enrolment · Greenhouse monthly, collected automatically · TOM in three instalments · speaking 50% on booking, 50% on delivery.

Run the market experiments

Test the buyer, the price and the message before betting on them — measure leaning-in and money, never politeness.

Run this play when
  • Before scaling a proposition or a price.
How to run it
  1. Buyer-language test — three fixed propositions, delivered flat, into CPO conversations; watch for leaning-in.
  2. Paid-willingness test — three real price points (~£5k / £15–25k / ~£50k) to three buyers.
  3. Pattern-resonance test — say the four compensation behaviours aloud; capture the verbatim "that's me".
Recommendation

Measure behaviour and money, never politeness. The experiments earn the right to a price, rather than assuming one.

The standing commercial gates and disclosures that keep the wares honest and the category defensible.
