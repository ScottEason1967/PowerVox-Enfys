The Deep Vault

One data-protection regime across two operations: the VQ Assessment beta and the Loop pattern library. This playbook is how each governance move is run — pick a group above, then a play. No beta invitation, and no class-B abstraction, until the gate is passed.

Honest data

The first dataset must be usable when the academic partner enters. Consent and pseudonymisation designed once, properly.

One regime, two classes

Class A personal beta data and class B engagement-derived patterns under a single architecture, not two.

The gate holds

External legal review and locked falsification criteria before anyone is invited. The gate is a feature, not a delay.

About this playbook

What the Deep Vault governs, who holds each line, and where the operational detail lives.

The two data classes

Class A — beta research data (personal): every participant from invitation to deletion — the VQ Assessment, the companion questionnaire (burnout-risk and psychological-safety items), the qualitative layer (free-text item, recorded debriefs, the optional mini-360), and the Greenhouse quarterly re-measures. Class B — pattern library data (engagement-derived): discovery-call recordings and transcripts (client staff are data subjects), client documents, and the abstracted, anonymised patterns retained by sector.

Roles & accountability · the RACI
  • Controller — PowerVox Ltd. Determines purposes and means for both classes. ⚑ confirm with reviewer
  • Consent / DP / IP owner — Scott. One architecture across both classes; external review the gate.
  • Pattern-library DP lead — Rachel. Class-B consent flow, pseudonymisation and privacy policy.
  • Research integrity — Hannah. Falsification criteria pre-set and held; the n=20 and n=60 gates; cave-month analysis.
  • External reviewer — to appoint. Independent legal check of the whole architecture before the first invitation.
  • Academic NED — to recruit. Arms-length oversight strengthening eventual published work.
Where the How lives

This playbook is the manual — the decisions and procedures, run play by play. How the work actually flows day to day is the operating model's How page, the Gather; the consent subsystem itself sits in the Burrows. Use those for the flow; use the plays here to run each governance move.

Recommendation · the drafter's position

Consent-led. Explicit consent is the clean, proportionate route at a ~100-person scale, and informed consent is required for research ethics regardless — the academic partner will expect it. The freely-given test holds because participants are paying clients, not employees. The respectable alternative is legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) as the general basis with explicit consent solely as the Article 9 condition and for the layers where consent is unavoidable; this yields a more stable dataset. Held open for the reviewer.

The contribution clause

Every Loop contract carries one uniform clause: the client licenses PowerVox to abstract anonymised, organisation-unidentifiable patterns into the library; PowerVox warrants the anonymisation standard and that no client document or identifiable material enters the library; the client's own model remains the client's.
