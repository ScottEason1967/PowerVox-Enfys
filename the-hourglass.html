The Hourglass

The Business Plan's own first sentence of strategy: "the constraint on growth remains time." The village counts its money carefully on the Wares and its rhythm beautifully on the Village Year. This page counts the days — what each keeper actually has, what the plan actually asks for, and where the two part company. Every figure is sourced to the Business Plan or flagged ⚑ where the documents are silent.

⚑ The key risk this page exists to hold

Rachel is on the critical path of every revenue line at once — cohort delivery, Greenhouse presence, Loop elicitation, speaking, BD and the signal that feeds Recognition — and the demand on her months at full plan runs roughly twice her protected envelope unless outside client days fall on schedule. There is no understudy anywhere in the model: a month of her absence stops every line simultaneously. The plan's own mitigations exist but are parked late — the associate-facilitator decision sits at months 13–18 and could be pulled forward; recorded and templated assets reduce live dependence; the open BD seat removes one load entirely when filled. The plan sharpens this: selling Root to Grow to three or four organisations for delivery between September and Christmas moves most of the cohort load into a single quarter, which makes the worked month above the norm for that quarter, not the peak.

The rule this page proposes ⚑ — no new commitment crosses the drawbridge without an Hourglass check, the same way no open item exists off the Noticeboard. The finish-before-you-start gate already protects Rachel from new ideas; the Hourglass protects the calendar from new yeses. The Village Clock (Burrows system 04) is where the rule would be enforced once confirmed; this page is where the arithmetic lives. The day-counts marked ⚑ are the Cartographer's working inferences and need keeper confirmation — the point is not the precision of the numbers, it is that the question "whose days does this take?" gets asked while no is still cheap.

Where it sits.

The Village Year is the rhythm; the Hourglass is the volume the rhythm has to carry. The Wares count what the work earns; the Hourglass counts what it costs in the only currency the village cannot mint. The substitution arc — outside days traded for product revenue — is the Business Plan's deliberate design; this page exists so the trade is watched, not assumed.
