The Wares

What the chassis sells — displayed in the village, owned by the company, never confused with how the village operates. One guiding metric governs the year: the PowerVox-product share of revenue, moving decisively upward — ~53% in Year 1, ~75% in Year 2, ~89% in Year 3 — as bespoke consultancy is deliberately traded out for the scaling funnel — the Millwheel.

The Black Egg · paid VQ tier →~£49–£99 (est.) · the one door inbuild · priority one

A standalone paid diagnostic that unlocks pattern recognition and a Voice Profile — already promised in the Black Egg copy. It closes the free-to-£1,495 pricing gap, becomes the scalable inbound product the plan depends on, and is an evidence on-ramp into the beta. One build, three strategic jobs: the single highest-leverage move on the board. Worked Year-1 contribution ~£15,000; 35–40 completions a quarter.

Owners: Hannah (product) · Scott (build/systems) · Rachel (signal/launch). In the village: the town crier on the path — dashed until it ships.
Root to Grow →£1,495 / participant · six weeksrun · the engine

The front of the funnel and the beta vehicle: initiation, awareness, language, baseline. Five conservative cohorts of 10–12 in Year 1 (~55 participants, ~£82,000), delivered live by Rachel and Hannah together — capacity, not appetite, sets the count. Every cohort runs the full VQ Assessment and Black Egg, so every pound also buys a row of beta evidence. Quality over volume: raving fans who convert onward at ≥60%.

Owners: Rachel (IP, design, delivery, coaching) · Hannah (methodology, delivery, research integrity) · Scott (operations, consent/IP). In the village: the planting, in the garden. See it run through the Gather →
The Greenhouse →~£125 / month · recurringrun · the destination

The destination of the funnel, the recurring-revenue engine, and the longitudinal research cohort — members re-take the diagnostic quarterly, turning a one-shot snapshot into change-over-time data on a held cohort. ~60% of Root to Grow alumni convert; ~35 members by year-end contributing ~£17.5k in-year but exiting at a ~£52.5k annual run-rate — the run-rate, not the in-year figure, is the asset. Also a named pathway in the Construct: the women's leadership sanctuary, alongside the Commons for allyship. The Year 2–3 unlock is direct membership without a cohort first, opened on a trigger, not a date.

Owners: Rachel (presence/community) · Scott (operations/retention). In the village: the glasshouse itself. See it run through the Gather →
the Loop →~£30k / engagement · capped at three bespokerun · the second engine

Paid target-operating-model delivery — the consulting arm's version of the move the whole business is making: founder-dependent delivery becoming an asset that earns while we deliver. Three engagements at ~£30k hold the Year-1 floor (~£90,000) while the count is deliberately capped and falling: bespoke is dropped only as Greenhouse run-rate covers it, roughly twenty stable members buying back one engagement. PowerVox is the rehearsal subject, a paid validation client the validation, a zero-knowledge client the falsification test. The method itself is the third thing — the instrument, not a TOM. The Loop product is part of PowerVox — PowerVox Ltd contracts, the IP stays in-house. Today, founder-delivered, its revenue counts as bespoke for the product-share metric; it is built to migrate to product as the method externalises. The working price is ~£30k; the trade name is still to be settled.

Owners: Scott (architecture, decision tools, persona-and-narrative) · Rachel (elicitation engine, playbook, templates) · Hannah (delivery support, assurance). In the village: the workshop barn. The method itself → · See it run through the Gather →
Coaching · the White Sofa →premium · founder-led · cappedrun · deliberately limited

Rachel's premium one-to-one coaching, the intimate counterpart to the stage. A real revenue line and her stated priority, but a founder service rather than a productised offer: it cannot be delivered without Rachel in the room, which is why it stays capped and pointed, every relationship routing back to the diagnostic. Speaking is not a separate ware. The founder's speaking is part of the signal that draws people in, so it lives with Recognition, not on this shelf. Counting it twice, as signal and as product, was the muddle this split removes.

Owner: Rachel. In the village: the White Sofa, the intimate seat. ⚑ The White Sofa is proposed canon, awaiting Rachel's pen.

The two assumptions to watch ⚑ — the price and the retention. Neither assumption is wrong; both are bets, and this strip exists so they stay visible rather than settled by silence.
